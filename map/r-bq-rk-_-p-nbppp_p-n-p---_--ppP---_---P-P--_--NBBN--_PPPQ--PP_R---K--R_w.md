The move played in the game here was O-O.
dxc5 is really interesting & highlighted in the video.

h4 was my instinct, either looking for an h-rook-augmented greek gift,
or just an attack in general.
Its clear, imo, that kingside aggression is warranted.
I wonder why White didn't go for that?
I guess they just didn't want that risky of a game.

I didn't like the look of the position after dxc5,
not seeing Bxh7 in that line.
Also, I saw ghosts with ...d4, which is dumb.
One of the ghosts I saw:

	1.dxc5 d5 2.Nxd5 Bxc5 3.Nxc6 Bxe3

.. and what I didn't see from this position is 4.Bxh7+!,
the idea being that if Black takes we get Qd3+ and win their queen.

In the end, though, 1...d5 just loses a pawn at best for black.

Anwyays, after dxc5 White gets to trade off their e3 bishop with tempo,
giving the queen space to maneuver.
Then, there's a bunch of ideas with Bxh7+ sacrificing the bishop,
but Black's pieces are just too far from the kingside.
Its greek-gifty but not that spefific setup.
