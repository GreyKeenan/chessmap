Task: Evaluate dxc5 here vs exf6.

exf6 looks really bad to me, positionally.
Black is cramped for space.
Rook takes or bishop takes or night takeseach put more pressure
on my then-less-stable center,
while also giving Black more space to maneuver.
The question, then, is whether there's some immediate tactic
to compensate after that move.
Or, is there some threat that I can only prevent with exf6?

If it were black to move, fxe6 is a bit scary looking.
White's center is going to be much less stable,
since there's so much pressure on e5.

1...fxe5 2.dxe5 loses to the fork.
1...fxe5 2.fxe5 cxd4 3.Bxd4
and the isolated e pawn seems likely to fall,
plus Black could grab the bishop pair, although they may not want to.
If 3...Nxd4, 4.Nxd4 drops the e pawn,
but white does have have Bxh7+ there, going up that important pawn.
I wouldn't dislike that line, but what worries me is if Bk doesnt take.

If 1.fxd6 looks bad, does dxc5 solve this issue?
1.dxc5 fxe5 anyways, 2.fxe5.
I don't think Bxh7 works here anymore
because Black has a slightly easier job getting their pieces over
and they have e4 as a resource.

In a game, I would probably just bring the a-rook over to the e or d file,
since it seems like Black is going to open things up.
Rad1 seems decent because then you have Rfe1 later,
but Rae1 now might be better because if Black doesn't open things up
its supporting the e pawn.
Rae1 would probably be my answer?
